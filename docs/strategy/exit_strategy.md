## 1. SaaS Valuation Context (2026 Market)

AI-native vertical SaaS companies are currently valued at:

| ARR Range | Revenue Multiple | Basis |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Pre-$1M (seed) | 20-40× ARR | If growth >100% YoY, strong retention |
| $1M-$5M (Series A) | 15-30× ARR | Net revenue retention >120%, usage-based expansion |
| $5M-$10M (Series B) | 10-20× ARR | Proven unit economics, multi-customer segments |
| $10M+ (growth) | 8-15× ARR | At scale, multiples compress toward efficiency metrics |

**Decision Studio's multiple boosters:**
- Usage-based pricing with strong NRR → higher multiples (investors reward 130%+ NRR)
- AI-native architecture (not AI-bolted-on) → premium over legacy SaaS
- Vertical with horizontal expansion potential → "land in finance, expand to ops/supply chain"
- Registry + methodology = defensible IP, not a GPT wrapper → survives "is this just a prompt?" due diligence
- VA trajectory data = measurable proof of value → de-risks renewal assumptions for acquirers

## 2. Exit Scenarios by ARR Trajectory

### Scenario A: Bootstrapped Acquisition (Month 24-36)
**Trigger:** Base case — $600K-$1.4M ARR

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| ARR at exit | $600K-$1.4M |
| Likely multiple | 8-15× (bootstrapped, <$2M ARR, but AI premium + usage-based) |
| **Valuation range** | **$5M-$21M** |
| Acquirer type | PE roll-up, mid-market analytics platform, consulting tech arm |

**Likely acquirers:**
- **PE roll-ups** (Vista Equity, Thoma Bravo scout deals) — assembling "analytics platform" portfolios. A $5M-$15M acquisition is a bolt-on.
- **Mid-market ERP/analytics vendors** (Sage, Epicor, Infor) — serve your exact ICP and have no decision intelligence layer. Decision Studio becomes a premium module.
- **Consulting tech arms** (Accenture Song, Deloitte Digital, PwC products group) — want to productize what they currently sell as labor.

**Founder outcome:** $5M-$21M, 100% equity (bootstrapped). Even at low end, meaningful outcome for solo founder.

### Scenario B: Funded Growth → Strategic Acquisition (Month 30-48)
**Trigger:** Upside case — $1.5M-$6M ARR, raise seed/Series A to accelerate

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| ARR at exit | $3M-$10M (with 12-18 months of funded growth post-raise) |
| Likely multiple | 15-25× (funded, fast-growing, AI vertical) |
| **Valuation range** | **$45M-$250M** |
| Acquirer type | Strategic platform (Salesforce, ServiceNow, Workday), large analytics player |

**Likely acquirers:**
- **Salesforce** — Has Tableau (BI) and Einstein (AI) but no decision intelligence workflow. Decision Studio fills the gap between "here's a dashboard" and "here's what to do about it."
- **ServiceNow** — Expanding from IT workflows into business operations. Decision intelligence is the natural next step after their AI agent platform.
- **Workday** — Financial planning + HCM. Decision Studio adds the "what happened, why, what to do" layer their finance customers ask for.
- **SAP** — Demo runs on SAP data. SAP's Joule AI assistant is generic; Decision Studio is the structured analytical layer Joule can't do.
- **Datadog/New Relic** (stretch) — They do infrastructure monitoring. Decision Studio is "business monitoring" — same pattern (detect → diagnose → resolve), different domain.

**Founder outcome:** 60-80% ownership post-raise (15-20% seed dilution, potential 10-15% Series A). At $100M valuation = $60M-$80M founder value.

### Scenario C: PE Portfolio Play → Platform Acquisition (Month 36-60)
**Trigger:** PE firm adopts across portfolio companies → you become infrastructure

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| ARR at exit | $5M-$15M (organic, PE portfolio expansion) |
| Likely multiple | 12-20× |
| **Valuation range** | **$60M-$300M** |
| Acquirer type | The PE firm itself (acqui-hire the platform), or strategic at higher multiple |

This is the scenario where the consulting partner strategy pays off. A PE firm that deploys Decision Studio across 5-10 portfolio companies doesn't want to lose the platform — they either acquire you or ensure you're locked in as a strategic vendor.

**Unique dynamics:**
- No VC dilution required — PE firm provides distribution, not capital
- PE firm may invest at favorable terms (strategic value to their portfolio > pure financial return)
- Exit could be PE firm acquiring 100% to keep platform in-house for portfolio operations

### Scenario D: Downside → Acqui-hire or Asset Sale (Month 24-30)
**Trigger:** $180K-$360K ARR, growth stalling

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Valuation range** | **$1M-$5M** (primarily IP + team expertise) |
| Acquirer type | AI startup needing domain expertise, consulting firm wanting tech capability |

Even in the downside, the registry architecture, analytical methodology pipeline, and VA system have IP value. A consulting firm that wants to productize its practice could buy the platform for less than the cost of building it.

## 3. The NRR Multiplier Effect

The single biggest factor in valuation multiple isn't ARR — it's **net revenue retention (NRR)**. This is where incremental pricing transforms the exit story:

| NRR | What It Means | Multiple Impact |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|
| <100% | Customers shrinking or churning | 5-8× (distressed) |
| 100-110% | Stable, minimal expansion | 8-12× |
| 110-130% | Healthy expansion (new KPIs, more assessments) | 12-20× |
| 130-150% | Strong expansion (multi-division, new domains) | 20-30× |
| >150% | Exceptional (Tier 1 → Tier 2 upgrades) | 25-40× |

**Under flat SaaS:** NRR is probably 100-110%. Customers pay the same every year unless you upsell them to a higher tier — which requires a sales conversation.

**Under incremental pricing:** NRR could be 130-150%+ naturally. A customer who starts with 15 KPIs and expands to 40 KPIs over 12 months — with no sales conversation, just organic "monitor this too" requests — grows from $90K to $145K ACV automatically. That's **161% NRR** on a single customer.

**This is the exit multiplier.** A $1M ARR company with 140% NRR is worth more than a $3M ARR company with 105% NRR, because the first company's revenue is accelerating while the second is flat.

### Illustrative Impact on Valuation

| ARR | NRR | Implied Multiple | Valuation |
|-----|-----|-----------------|-----------|
| $1M | 105% (flat SaaS) | 10× | $10M |
| $1M | 140% (incremental) | 22× | $22M |
| $3M | 105% (flat SaaS) | 12× | $36M |
| $3M | 140% (incremental) | 25× | $75M |

The incremental pricing model doesn't just increase revenue — it fundamentally changes the valuation category.

## 5. What to Optimize Now for Exit Value

### Track from Day 1

1. **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** — Even with 2 customers, calculate and report monthly. This is the number acquirers and investors ask for first.

2. **KPI expansion rate** — When a customer adds their 11th KPI, log it. The story of "every customer expands by 40%+ in Year 1" is worth millions in valuation.

3. **Time-to-value** — Days from onboarding start to first actionable situation card. Target: <5 days (Fast Start). Short time-to-value = low churn risk = higher multiples.

4. **VA trajectory data** — Every chart showing "our recommendation improved this KPI by X%" is due diligence gold. It's the proof that the product works → customers renew → acquirers pay for certainty.

### Architecture Decisions That Affect Exit Value

5. **Multi-tenant isolation** — Even with 3 customers, the architecture should show acquirers it can handle 300. Already partially in place (registry-per-customer pattern).

6. **Domain-agnostic demonstrations** — Show it working in finance AND operations AND supply chain (even demo data). Acquirers pay more for horizontal potential than vertical depth.

7. **API-first design** — Acquirers want to integrate, not rebuild. The existing FastAPI + standardized agent protocol + Pydantic I/O makes integration straightforward.

### Decisions That Gate Exit Paths

| Decision | Opens Path | Closes Path |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Stay bootstrapped | A (full equity), C (PE portfolio) | B (need funding to reach $3M+ ARR fast) |
| Raise seed at $1M ARR | B (strategic acquisition) | Dilutes A outcome by 15-20% |
| Take strategic investment (SAP/Salesforce Ventures) | B (inside track to strategic acq) | C (PE path complicated by strategic investor) |
| PE portfolio deal (no dilution) | C (strongest path) | B (PE relationship may limit strategic sale) |

## 6. Recommended Exit Strategy Sequence

**Phase 1 (Now → Month 12): Build for Scenario A, position for B or C**
- Optimize for NRR and KPI expansion metrics
- Stay bootstrapped — preserve 100% equity
- Build relationships with PE firms through Tier 0/1 partner strategy (consulting partners)
- Every architecture decision should be "would this survive due diligence?"

**Phase 2 (Month 12-18): Decide funding path**
- If $600K+ ARR with 130%+ NRR → consider seed raise to accelerate (Scenario B)
- If PE firm is deploying across portfolio → deepen that relationship (Scenario C)
- If <$300K ARR → focus on retention and expansion, not fundraising

**Phase 3 (Month 18-36): Execute chosen path**
- Scenario A: Engage M&A advisor at $1M ARR. Target PE roll-ups and ERP vendors.
- Scenario B: Raise Series A at $3M+ ARR. Build toward strategic acquisition at $5M-$10M.
- Scenario C: Negotiate PE platform deal. Acquisition or exclusive long-term contract.

**The key insight:** You don't need to choose an exit path now. The actions that maximize Scenario A (high NRR, clean metrics, defensible IP) also maximize Scenarios B and C. The decision point comes at Month 12-18, informed by actual customer data.
