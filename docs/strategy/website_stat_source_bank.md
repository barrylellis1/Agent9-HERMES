# Website Stat Source Bank

**Last updated:** 2026-06-25
**Purpose:** Source-safe statistics for Decision Studio website, sales pages, and outbound collateral. Use official/public sources first. Treat secondary reporting as internal context unless a primary link is verified.

## Recommended Public Stats

### 1. AI adoption is broad, but enterprise value is not

**Site-safe copy:** McKinsey's 2025 State of AI survey found that 88% of respondents report regular AI use in at least one business function, but only 39% report enterprise-level EBIT impact.

**Source:** McKinsey, "The state of AI in 2025: Agents, innovation, and transformation"
https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai

**Supports:** AI investment gap, proof gap, board pressure to show P&L impact.

**Best placement:** Landing page "decision execution gap"; insights page "accountability reckoning"; sales deck opening problem slide.

**Confidence:** High. Public primary source.

### 2. AI high performers are still rare

**Site-safe copy:** McKinsey identifies AI high performers as about 6% of respondents, defined by significant value and at least 5% EBIT impact attributable to AI.

**Source:** McKinsey, "The state of AI in 2025: Agents, innovation, and transformation"
https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai

**Supports:** Category urgency. The market is using AI, but very few companies have an operating model that captures value.

**Best placement:** Long-form insight article or sales narrative, not hero copy.

**Confidence:** High. Public primary source.

### 3. Workflow redesign, validation, and KPI tracking correlate with value

**Site-safe copy:** McKinsey reports that AI high performers are more likely to redesign workflows, define human-validation processes, and track KPIs for AI solutions.

**Source:** McKinsey, "The state of AI in 2025: Agents, innovation, and transformation"
https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai

**Supports:** Decision Studio's architecture: human-in-the-loop review, structured workflows, KPI-linked Value Assurance.

**Best placement:** How It Works, trust model, architecture explainer.

**Confidence:** High. Public primary source.

## Secondary Or Unverified Candidate Stats

### Gartner: GenAI project abandonment after proof of concept

**Candidate copy:** Gartner has been widely reported as predicting that at least 30% of GenAI projects would be abandoned after proof of concept by the end of 2025 because of poor data quality, inadequate risk controls, escalating costs, or unclear business value.

**Public secondary source:** TechRadar, "What might the AI-fueled finance department of tomorrow look like?"
https://www.techradar.com/pro/what-might-the-ai-fueled-finance-department-of-tomorrow-look-like

**Use guidance:** Do not put this on the website as a Gartner claim unless we verify the original Gartner newsroom/report link or you provide licensed Gartner material. It is useful for internal messaging because it matches the Decision Studio value thesis.

**Confidence:** Medium. Secondary source currently verified; primary Gartner source not yet found.

### MIT NANDA: GenAI pilot failure / no measurable ROI

**Candidate copy:** MIT NANDA's "GenAI Divide" has been widely reported as finding that 95% of enterprise GenAI pilots show no measurable P&L impact.

**Public secondary examples:** Business Insider, Windows Central, Tom's Hardware, Times of India.

**Use guidance:** This is highly quotable but also very sharp. Avoid public-site use until the original MIT/NANDA report URL is verified. If used later, frame it as "reported by" with caution.

**Confidence:** Medium. Secondary sources are visible, but primary source was not verified in this pass.

## Recommended Messaging Pattern

Use the research to support three claims:

1. AI adoption is already widespread.
2. Measurable enterprise value remains rare.
3. The winners are not the companies with the most AI demos. They are the ones that redesign workflows, add human validation, and track KPI-level outcomes.

That pattern is exactly where Decision Studio is strongest: detect, diagnose, debate, approve, prove.
