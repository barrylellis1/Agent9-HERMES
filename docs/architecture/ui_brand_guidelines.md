# Agent9 / Decision Studio: Brand & UI Guidelines

**Created:** 2026-04-04
**Scope:** Unified design system governing both the marketing landing page (`decision-studios.com`) and the core application (`trydecisionstudio.com`).

---

## 1. The Core Aesthetic: "Swiss Style" AI

Decision Studio completely avoids the flashy, chatty, "sparkly" trends of typical SaaS AI tools. The platform is designed for C-suite executives managing millions of dollars in KPI variances. The interface must project absolute authority and competence.

**Visual Rules:**
- **Vibe:** Precise, intelligent, calm, trustworthy, and understated.
- **Color Palette:** High-contrast monochrome dominance (deep slates, charcoal, and stark white). Color is treated as a scarce resource used strictly for semantic meaning:
  - *Red/Green:* Financial truth (Variance/Opportunity).
  - *Subtle Blue:* AI intervention, recommended action, or system processing.
- **Typography:** Clean, editorial sans-serif (*Satoshi*). We rely on clear typographic hierarchy rather than boxes and borders.
- **Structure:** Progressive disclosure. The UI should feel like a meticulously organized "analytical binder" sliding across a boardroom table. Heavy use of clean horizontal lines and collapsible accordions ensures the screen is never overwhelming.

---

## 2. The Brand Voice: "The Quiet Expert"

Agent9 acts like a Senior Partner at an elite consulting firm (McKinsey, BCG, Bain)—without the ego or billable hours. 

- **Objective & Direct:** We deliver the Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF). We present data, trade-offs, and probabilities. We do not preach.
- **No Alarmism:** Even if a KPI is bleeding millions, the system states the variance factually. No exclamation points or panic.
- **Understated:** We never use words like "Magic," "Awesome," or "Revolutionize." The depth of the analysis proves the system's intelligence.

### Adaptive Contextualization
While the calm tone never changes, the *framing* adapts to the Principal logged in:
- **Analytical CFO:** Focuses on root-cause breakdowns, risk quantification, and MECE logic.
- **Pragmatic COO:** Focuses on operational realities, quick wins, and implementation timelines.
- **Visionary CEO:** Focuses on portfolio strategy, market positioning, and long-term value chain impact.

---

## 3. The UX Philosophy: "The Chart is the Receipt"

Decision Studio explicitly avoids the **"BI Trap."** We are an AI Decision Intelligence platform, not a BI tool.

- **Narrative over Charts:** A traditional BI tool asks the human to look at a chart and figure out what it means. Agent9 does the math, isolates the variance, and tells the story (using SCQA: Situation, Complication, Question, Answer).
- **Proof, Not Playground:** The visualizations are crisp and static-first. They do not have drill-down buttons or open-ended filter dropdowns. The chart acts simply as a "receipt" proving the AI did the math correctly. If an executive wants to drill down, they ask the AI a question via the Refinement Chat.

---

## 4. The Logo: "The Aperture"

Our logomark is the exact opposite of current AI trends (no brains, no sparkles, no 3D gradients). It is a strict, brutalist, geometric shape.

- **The Mark:** A stark "focus box" or viewfinder with a heavy outer frame and a solid central core.
- **The Meaning:** It visually represents filtering out the noise of enterprise data to zero in on the definitive truth (the root cause).
- **The Subtext:** Subconsciously, it aligns the brand with high-end architecture, elite publishing, or private equity, projecting stability and absolute trust.

---

## 5. Unified Experience (Marketing to Product)

One of the biggest mistakes enterprise SaaS companies make is creating a disconnect between the marketing site and the actual product. 

Because the Landing Page (`decision-studios.com`) and the Application (`trydecisionstudio.com`) live in the exact same React/Tailwind codebase:
- **Zero Disconnect:** The transition from prospect to user is completely seamless.
- **Consistent Delivery:** The marketing site uses the exact same dark slate backgrounds, Aperture logo, and "Quiet Expert" typography that they will see in their first Executive Briefing. 
- **Message Alignment:** The landing page copy focuses on business outcomes (*"Decisions ready in hours, not quarters"*, Value Assurance, Cost of Inaction) rather than AI features.
